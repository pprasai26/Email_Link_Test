No credentials were collected. This page exists to help you identify and report suspicious emails in the future.

What happened?

You clicked a link in a simulated phishing email used for PUBP 6725 Assignment 1. The goal is to practice recognizing red flags in a low‑risk environment. Nothing was captured: no usernames, passwords, cookies, or personal data.

How to spot it next time

1. Inspect the sender: Look for misspelled domains or unexpected aliases. Example: it-support@unlversity.edu (the “l” is actually an “I”).
2. Hover before you click: Check the real destination URL matches the site you expect.
3. Watch the tone: Urgency, threats, or “immediate action required” are common social‑engineering tactics.
4. Unexpected attachments/links: Confirm with the sender via a known-good channel before opening.
5. MFA and passwords: Never enter credentials after following a link from an email; instead, navigate directly to the official site.
See the subtle clues used in the simulation
  • A generic greeting and unusual sender address.
  • A call-to-action button with a non-university URL.
  • Time pressure (“review pending”).
